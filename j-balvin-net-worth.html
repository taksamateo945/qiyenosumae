Biography

J Balvin, born on May 7, 1985, hails from Medellin, Colombia, the same city associated with the notorious drug lord Pablo Escobar.

Originally named Jose Alvaro Rosario Balwin, he gained fame under the stage name J Balvin.

His music transcends language barriers, as he is known for his popular Spanish and English songs.

Despite not being a native English speaker, his English performances have captivated audiences worldwide.

At the age of 17, J Balvin made a significant move to the United States alongside his best friend. Today, he stands as the highest-earning Latin Pop artist in the industry.

His songs have achieved remarkable success, with several of them reigning at the top of Billboard’s Hot Latin Songs chart for an impressive duration of approximately thirty-seven weeks.

For nearly five years, English movies have featured his songs. One notable example is his song remix, which was included in Fast & Furious 7.

His popularity can be measured by the fact that his song has surpassed 1 billion views on YouTube.

He actively engages in collaborations with other music stars, such as Ariana Grande, Maroon 5, and Robin Thicke.

J Balvin Net Worth and Career

In 2004, he embarked on his music career and introduced his debut album, Real, in 2010.

The release of his single “6 AM” in 2014, featuring Puerto Rican singer Farruko, propelled him to mainstream success.

This song reached number 2 on the Billboard Hot Latin Songs chart.

Balvin has since unleashed a series of hit singles such as “Ay Vamos,” “Ginza,” “Bobo,” “Safari,” and “Mi Gente.”

Additionally, he has collaborated with various artists, including Beyoncé, Cardi B, The Black Eyed Peas, Nicky Jam, and Major Lazer.

The BMI Latin Awards recognized Balvin as the Contemporary Latin Songwriter of the Year in 2017.

Moreover, he has amassed an impressive collection of accolades, including six Latin Grammy Awards, eleven Billboard Latin Music Awards, and five MTV Video Music Awards.

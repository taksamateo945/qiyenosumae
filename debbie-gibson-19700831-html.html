Deborah Ann Gibson is an American singer, songwriter, record producer and actress. Gibson released their debut album Out of the Blue in 1987, which produced several international hits, and was subsequently certified three platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America. One of those singles, "Foolish Beat," made Gibson the youngest female artist to write, produce, and perform on Billboard's number one single Hot 100. Her second platinum album, Electric Youth, gave Gibson a number one hit in the United States with "Lost in Your Eyes". Gibson is the only musician of all her singles to make the Top 20 on the Hot 100 chart. She was recognized by ASCAP as Musician of the Year, alongside Bruce Springsteen, in 1989.
